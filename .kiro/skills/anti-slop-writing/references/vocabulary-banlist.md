# AI Vocabulary Ban List (English)

Complete list of English words and phrases to avoid, organized by category, AI model era, and signal strength. Based on Kobak et al. (2025, Science Advances), the Antislop Framework (Paech et al., ICLR 2026), Wikipedia WikiProject AI Cleanup, ContentBeta 300+ word analysis (2026), and community tracking across 50,000+ patterns.

---

## By AI Model Era

### 2023 to mid-2024 (GPT-4 era)
Additionally, boasts, bolstered, crucial, delve/delves/delving, emphasizing, enduring, garner, intricate/intricacies, interplay, key (adjective), landscape (figurative), meticulous/meticulously, pivotal, underscore, tapestry, testament, valuable, vibrant

### Mid-2024 to mid-2025 (GPT-4o / Claude 3.5 era)
Align with, bolstered, crucial, emphasizing, enhance, enduring, fostering, highlighting, pivotal, showcasing, underscore, vibrant. "Delve" started declining. More sophisticated hedging emerged.

### Mid-2025 to 2026 (GPT-5 / Claude 4 / Gemini 2.5 era)
Emphasizing, enhance, highlighting, showcasing, plus words associated with notability/attribution emphasis. Models became subtler — vocabulary tells weakened but structural tells strengthened. "Delve" dropped off sharply. Detection shifted from single-word signals to sentence-level and paragraph-level patterns.

## Signal Strength Tiers

### Tier 1 — Strongest AI Signals (10-50x overrepresentation vs. human baseline)
These words appear 10 to 50 times more frequently in LLM output than in human writing. Three or more in a single paragraph will almost certainly trigger detection.

delve, tapestry, testament, underscore, pivotal, meticulous/meticulously, intricate/intricacies, landscape (abstract), vibrant, crucial, foster/fostering, garner, showcase/showcasing, bolster/bolstered, nuanced, multifaceted, comprehensive, robust, seamless

### Tier 2 — Strong Signals (4-10x overrepresentation)
utilize (instead of "use"), facilitate, implement, optimize, streamline, enhance, endeavor, commence, ascertain, elucidate, paramount, plethora, myriad, trajectory, spectrum, framework, methodology, ecosystem, stakeholders, actionable, scalable, cutting-edge, game-changer, empower, curate, elevate, harness, spearhead, cultivate, mitigate, proliferation, juxtaposition, underpinning, confluence, commendable

### Tier 3 — Moderate Signals (context-dependent, 2-4x overrepresentation)
furthermore, moreover, notably, indeed, crucially, excitingly, realm, interplay, resonate, navigate (abstract), unlock, unpack, leverage, deep dive, holistic, dynamic, innovative, transformative, groundbreaking

### Copula-Avoidance Constructions
AI substitutes elaborate verb phrases for simple "is/are/has."
- serves as (a) → use "is"
- stands as (a) → use "is"
- marks (a) → describe directly
- represents (a shift/change) → use "is" or describe directly
- boasts (meaning "has") → use "has"
- features (meaning "has") → use "has"
- offers (meaning "has") → use "has"
- holds the distinction of → use "is"
- ventured into → use "entered" or "became"
- emerged as → usually just "is" or "became"
- constitutes → "is" or "makes up"

### Formal Connectives (replace with simple alternatives)
- furthermore → also, and
- moreover → also, and
- consequently → so
- accordingly → so
- nonetheless → still, but
- nevertheless → still, but
- henceforth → from now on
- thereby → this way
- wherein → where, in which
- thus → so
- hence → so
- additionally → also, and
- in addition → also

### Common AI Filler Phrases (cut or replace)
- "plays a [crucial/key/important] role" → state the action directly
- "when it comes to" → rewrite with a verb
- "in order to" → "to"
- "a wide range of" → name what's in the range
- "needless to say" → cut
- "it goes without saying" → cut
- "more often than not" → "usually" or give a number
- "take a closer look at" → cut or be direct
- "at this point in time" → "now"
- "in recent years" → give the actual years or timeframe
- "this comprehensive guide" → cut "comprehensive"
- "dive deep into" → just discuss it
- "navigate the complexities of" → name the specific complexities
- "at its core" → cut or state what it actually is

### Formulaic AI Sentence Patterns (never use)
- "It's not just X, it's Y" / "It's not about X, it's about Y"
- "That's not X, that's Y"
- "Not only X, but also Y"
- "No X. No Y. Just Z." (staccato triplet)
- "Whether you're [X] or [Y], [generic statement]"
- "In an era where [broad trend], [obvious observation]"
- "From [X] to [Y], [sweeping generalization]"

### Formulaic AI Pairs (don't use together)
AI reflexively pairs these concepts. Don't use them as a set:
- "challenges and opportunities"
- "on one hand... on the other hand..."
- "pros and cons"
- "strengths and weaknesses"
- "risks and rewards"

### Excessive Hedging (replace with direct statements or genuine uncertainty)
- "It is worth noting that" → cut; state the fact
- "It could be argued that" → take a position
- "There are several factors to consider" → name the factors
- "This suggests that" → state the conclusion
- "In many cases" → give a number or say "usually"

### Fake Authenticity Signals (never use)
AI has learned to mimic casual voice with these constructions, but they're now recognized as AI tells:
- "But honestly?"
- "Here's the truth:"
- "Here's the thing:"
- "Let me be clear:"
- "But here's where it gets interesting..."
- "Think about it this way..."
- "Sure! Here's..."
- "Let me break this down..."

### Collaborative Chat Artifacts (never include)
- "I hope this helps!"
- "Of course!"
- "Certainly!"
- "You're absolutely right!"
- "Would you like me to..."
- "Is there anything else..."
- "Let me know if..."

## Model-Specific Vocabulary Tells

### ChatGPT-Specific (GPT-4/4o/5)
Heavy use of: delve (pre-2025), tapestry, testament, landscape, em dashes, numbered lists, bold formatting, "In today's fast-paced world," enthusiastic/promotional tone, "Let's dive in"

### Claude-Specific (Anthropic)
Heavy use of: epistemic hedging ("I think," "There's a case to be made"), "serves as," "stands as," copula avoidance, measured/cautious tone, "nuanced," over-qualified statements, balanced both-sides framing

### Gemini-Specific (Google)
Heavy use of: purple prose, excessive adjectives, moralizing, explicit theme statements, documentation-like structure, textbook tone, "It's important to..."

## Replacement Strategy

When you encounter a banned word, don't just swap it for a synonym. Restructure the sentence to say what you actually mean in plain language. AI writing fails not because of wrong words, but because sentences fill space without conveying new information.

**Before (AI):**
> The festival serves as a vibrant testament to the region's rich cultural heritage, showcasing the intricate tapestry of traditions that have endured through the ages, contributing to the broader social fabric of the community.

**After (human):**
> The festival has been running since 1987. Locals sell handmade pottery and goat cheese from stalls they build themselves each spring.

**Before (AI):**
> In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, innovative companies are leveraging cutting-edge technologies to navigate the complexities of an increasingly interconnected marketplace.

**After (human):**
> Shopify added AI product descriptions in March 2025. Sales for stores using it went up 12%.

**Before (AI):**
> It's not just about the technology — it's about the people. From startups to Fortune 500 companies, organizations are discovering that the key to success lies in fostering a culture of innovation and collaboration.

**After (human):**
> Three companies tried this. Two failed. Basecamp made it work because they let teams pick their own tools instead of mandating one platform.

The fix is never finding a better adjective. The fix is replacing vague praise with specific facts.

## Words Humans Use That AI Rarely Produces

### Discourse Markers (inject these naturally)
- Processing markers: "Well," "I mean," "Look," "So," "Right," "Okay so," "The thing is"
- Hedging: "I think," "sort of," "kind of," "arguably," "in a way," "I suppose," "as far as I can tell"
- Concessive: "Fair enough," "Granted," "Mind you," "That said," "To be fair"
- Stance: "Honestly," "Frankly," "Personally," "Admittedly"
- Self-correction: "No, wait—" "Actually," "Or rather," "Let me rephrase that"
- Epistemic: "from what I gather," "if I'm not mistaken," "last I checked"

### Informal Intensifiers
pretty much, basically, literally (casual), kind of, sort of, way (too much), super, totally, absolutely, seriously

### Casual Connectors (use instead of AI transitions)
And, But, So, Plus, Also, Still, Though, Anyway, Besides, Meanwhile, Then again

### Sentence Fragments Humans Use
- Single-word: "Exactly." "Obviously." "Nope." "Right."
- Incomplete thoughts: "Not ideal." "Big difference." "Same old story."
- Afterthoughts: "Which is saying something." "Not that it matters." "For better or worse."
- Emphasis: "Every. Single. Time." "No exceptions."
