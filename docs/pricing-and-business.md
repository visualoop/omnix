# Pricing & Business Model — SokoOS

## Target Customer
- Single-location pharmacies in Kenya
- 1-5 staff members
- Monthly revenue: KES 200K-600K
- Currently using: Excel, paper, basic POS, or nothing
- NOT targeting: chains, hospitals, distributors

## Pricing Model: License + Annual Maintenance

```
One-time license:         KES 30,000  (buy once, own forever)
Annual maintenance fee:   KES 12,000/year  (KES 1,000/month equivalent)
Additional POS device:    KES 10,000  (one-time, per extra terminal on LAN)
```

### What the License Includes
- Full SokoOS:Dawa system (all features)
- 1 device activation
- First year of maintenance included (so first payment = KES 30,000 total)
- Free installation support (remote)
- Free data migration from Excel/CSV

### What Annual Maintenance Covers (recurring revenue)
- KRA eTIMS updates (KRA changes API specs — this WILL happen)
- SHA/SHIF insurance integration updates
- Bug fixes and security patches
- New features and improvements
- WhatsApp/phone support (business hours)
- Free data recovery assistance

### What Happens If They Don't Renew
- Software keeps working (never locks them out)
- No more updates (eTIMS might break when KRA changes things)
- No support
- They can renew anytime (no penalty)
- This is fair and builds trust

### Why This Creates Recurring Revenue
KRA and SHA change their systems regularly. Pharmacies NEED updates to stay compliant.
Natural renewal pressure without being predatory:
- "Your eTIMS integration needs updating for the new KRA spec"
- "SHA changed their claims format — update available"
- Pharmacy renews because the alternative is non-compliance

## Launch Strategy

### Phase 1: First 5 Customers (Validation)
- Price: KES 20,000 (early adopter discount, maintenance free for Year 1)
- Find: personal network, walk into pharmacies in your area
- Goal: prove it works in real pharmacy environment
- Get: testimonials + referrals

### Phase 2: Growth (5-35 customers)
- Price: Full KES 30,000 + KES 12K/year (first year included)
- Channel: WhatsApp groups (pharmacist communities), pharmacy school alumni, PPB events
- Offer: free 7-day trial (install, use, decide)
- Referral: existing customer gets 1 month free maintenance per referral

### Phase 3: Scale (35+ customers)
- Price: Hold at KES 30,000
- Channel: Pharmaceutical reps (they visit every pharmacy), Facebook/Google ads
- Consider: partner with pharmaceutical distributors (bundle software with supply deals)

---

## What NOT to Do
- Don't give free trials longer than 7 days (enough to test, not enough to abuse)
- Don't discount below KES 20,000 (perceived as cheap/unreliable)
- Don't promise "lifetime free updates" (you'll regret it)
- Don't target chains yet (their needs are complex, support overhead is high)
- Don't compete on price alone (compete on: offline reliability + data ownership + lower total cost)
